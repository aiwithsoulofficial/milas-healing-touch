Module 03

The Scientific / Physical Side of Candles

Understanding what physically happens when a candle burns

"Even physically, the candle is a perfect metaphor for transformation. The solid becomes liquid. The liquid becomes vapour. The vapour becomes flame. Spiritually, this mirrors what happens in healing: what is dense begins to soften, move, release, and transform."

- Mila Velazquez
The physical process of combustion
  1. 1
    Flame melts wax Heat radiates from the flame, liquefying the solid wax around the wick
  2. 2
    Liquid wax travels up the wick Capillary action draws the liquid fuel upward through the braided wick
  3. 3
    Heat vaporises the wax The liquid wax at the top of the wick is converted to gas by intense heat
  4. 4
    Vapour reacts with oxygen This combustion reaction creates heat and light - the visible flame
Basic combustion formula

Fuel + Heat + Oxygen = Heat + Light - Flames move due to convection, rising hot air creates the teardrop shape we recognise.

Why flames move - holding both truths
Physical causes

Air currents, drafts, uneven wick, impurities in the wax, moisture content, room temperature changes

Metaphysical causes

Energetic activity, emotional resistance, spirit communication, clearing in progress, energy shifting

"Not every dancing flame means spirit is talking. Sometimes your AC vent is talking. We have to learn how to hold both truths: the physical world and the energetic world. Discernment is spiritual maturity."

- Mila Velazquez

Module 04

How to Read Candle Flames

Symbolic language - not fear, not obsession, but noticing patterns

Key teaching point: Candle reading is symbolic language. It is not about fear, obsession, or rigid rules. It is about noticing patterns, energy, and intuitive messages.

Flame interpretation reference chart
Flame Behaviour Metaphysical Meaning Message Physical Cause
Strong, steady Aligned energy, clear intention, strong spiritual support "The path is clear. Energy is stable." Good wick, still air
Tall flame Strong energy, heightened spiritual activity, amplified intention, emotional intensity "This work is active." Wick too long, excess fuel
Small or weak Low momentum, fatigue, emotional heaviness, resistance, blocked intention "Energy may need more clarity, support, or clearing." Short wick, poor oxygen, wax drowning wick
Flickering / dancing Energetic movement, spiritual communication, resistance shifting "Something is moving." Airflow, room movement
Popping / crackling Emotional charge, energetic resistance, "something wants to be heard", tension releasing "Listen." Trapped moisture, debris, wick impurities
Goes out unexpectedly Resistance, timing not aligned, energy needs reassessment, spiritual pause "Pause. Reassess. Clear first." Drowning wick, no oxygen, draft

Remember: A candle going out is not automatically a curse, attack, or doom. Sometimes it means pause. Sometimes it means clear first. Sometimes it means the work needs to be approached differently.

How to Choose the Right Candle

Intention first, then colour, then form

Choosing a candle is an intuitive act. Start with your intention, then let the colour and form follow. There is no wrong choice - only conscious choice.

  1. 1
    Start with Intention Ask yourself: What am I calling in? What am I releasing? What support do I need? The answer to this shapes everything else.
  2. 2
    Match the Colour Use the colour reference chart (Module 6) to match your intention to a colour frequency. Trust what you are drawn to - your intuition often knows before your mind does.
  3. 3
    Choose the Candle Shape/Type Match the form to the ritual type and duration. Short work uses short candles. Long-term intentions need sustained burn times. See Module 8 for types.

How to Prepare a Candle Energetically

Cleanse, program, anoint - imprinting consciousness into form

"You are not just lighting wax. You are imprinting consciousness into form."

- Mila Velazquez
  1. 1
    Step 1 - Cleanse the candle Clear any residual energy from the candle before use. Methods include: prayer, breath, smoke cleansing (sage, palo santo), sound (singing bowl, clapping), intention, Reiki or energy work, or simply holding it in your hands and setting the intention to clear.
  2. 2
    Step 2 - Program the candle Hold the candle in your hands. State clearly what the candle is for. Speak what you are releasing. Speak what you are calling in. Ask Spirit, God, Source, or your guides to support the work. This is where consciousness meets form.
  3. 3
    Step 3 - Optional anointing Apply a small amount of oil to the candle with intention. Anoint top to bottom for release work (sending energy out). Anoint bottom to top for drawing in. Toward you = calling in. Away from you = releasing.
Anointing direction quick reference

Top to Bottom (away from you)
= Release, banish, let go, clear

Bottom to Top (toward you)
= Draw in, manifest, attract, heal

Spiritual Discernment

Tools support your healing - they don't replace it

"A candle can support your healing, but it is not here to bypass your healing. Ritual should deepen your awareness - not replace your inner work."

- Mila Velazquez

Candles are powerful tools. But they are tools. They are not replacements for the deeper work of healing, regulation, and self-responsibility. Part of spiritual maturity is knowing the difference.

Candles are NOT replacements for
Nervous System Regulation Breathwork, somatic practices, and grounding your body
Trauma Healing Professional support for deep wounds that need clinical care
Therapy Ongoing emotional processing with a trained professional
Medical Care Physical symptoms that require diagnosis and treatment
Emotional Responsibility Doing your own inner work and taking ownership
Practical Action The real-world steps that manifestation requires

Spiritual bypassing is using spiritual practices to avoid dealing with emotional pain, unresolved trauma, or practical responsibilities. A candle ritual before a difficult conversation is beautiful. A candle ritual instead of a difficult conversation is avoidance.

Module 12

Fire Safety

We honour the element of fire by respecting it

"We honour the element of fire by respecting it."

- Mila Velazquez
Essential fire safety rules
  • Never leave a candle unattended - this is non-negotiable, regardless of ritual
  • Keep away from flammable materials - curtains, paper, loose fabric, pets, children
  • Use a stable, heat-safe surface - ceramic plate, fire-safe tray, stone or tile
  • Trim wick to 1/4 inch before use - reduces soot, prevents tall flames, cleaner burn
  • Avoid heavy drafts - uneven burning, dripping, and fire hazard
  • Don't burn too long - follow the candle manufacturer's recommended burn time
  • Use discernment with herbs, oils, and dressings - dried herbs and oils are flammable; keep additions minimal and away from the wick
  • Extinguish consciously and safely - use a snuffer or pinch; blowing can scatter hot wax or sparks
Remember

Sacred practice and practical safety are not in conflict. You can honour the spiritual significance of fire and respect its physical power at the same time. That is the whole point of discernment.
