What is Muscle Testing?

How the body communicates truth through its energy system

Muscle testing - also called applied kinesiology - is a way of communicating with the subconscious mind through the body's energy system. The premise is simple: your body responds differently to true versus false information.

When something is true or resonant

Your body stays strong. Energy flows. The electrical circuit holds. There is a "yes" in the nervous system.

When something is false or incongruent

Your body weakens. The circuit disrupts. The nervous system signals "no" before the conscious mind can intervene.

This is not a belief system. It is physics. Your body is an electrical system - every cell, every organ, every muscle runs on bioelectric current. That current is influenced by the information it processes, including your thoughts, words, foods, and the trapped emotions sitting in your energy field.

The Autonomic Nervous System Connection

The autonomic nervous system governs involuntary responses - heart rate, digestion, muscle tone. It responds before the conscious mind catches up. When you muscle test, you are tapping into this system. Research in psychoneuroimmunology shows that emotional states directly affect muscle strength and neurological function. Your body has been tracking truth this entire time. Muscle testing gives you the interface to read it.

Who can learn this?

Everyone. You do not need to be a practitioner. You do not need any prior experience. You need a body and the willingness to listen to it. That is it.

Method 01

The Sway Test

Where most people start - and where many people stay

The sway test is gentle, solo, and remarkably clear once you stop second-guessing it.

How to do it
  1. 1
    Stand with your feet hip-width apart, arms relaxed at your sides. Take three slow breaths and let your body settle.
  2. 2
    Close your eyes if that helps. Feel yourself rooted but loose - not locked at the knees.
  3. 3
    Say something you know to be true out loud or in your mind. Your full name, your date of birth, "I am alive."
  4. 4
    Notice: your body will gently move forward. That is your YES.
  5. 5
    Now say something false - a wrong name, a wrong date. Feel the body shift backward. That is your NO.
  6. 6
    Once you have your baseline, you can start asking real questions.

↓
Backward = No

Tips for accuracy
  • Remove shoes if possible - more grounded
  • Drink water before you start
  • Always establish yes/no baseline first
  • Keep questions simple and specific
Common mistakes
  • Anticipating the answer and leaning
  • Asking vague or double-barrelled questions
  • Testing when exhausted or dehydrated
  • Testing the same question obsessively

Method 02

Arm Test & Finger Test

Classic kinesiology and the solo O-ring technique

Partner Arm Test

Classic applied kinesiology. One person tests, one person holds a substance, thought, or statement.

  1. 1
    Tester stands to the side of the subject. Subject extends dominant arm parallel to the floor.
  2. 2
    Tester places two fingers on the subject's wrist. The other hand rests lightly on the subject's opposite shoulder.
  3. 3
    Subject holds the item or states the statement. Takes a breath.
  4. 4
    Tester says "Hold" and applies gentle, steady downward pressure for 2 seconds.
  5. 5
    Strong arm = YES. Arm drops easily = NO. Aim for consistent, not forceful pressure.

Tester tip: You are not trying to push the arm down. You are listening for the response. Calibrate on a true statement first - feel what strong feels like for this person's body.

Arm extended, gentle pressure
Solo Finger Test (O-Ring)

The O-ring technique means you test yourself - no partner needed. This is the method Mila uses most in daily life.

  1. 1
    Touch your thumb and index finger together on your non-dominant hand to form an "O".
  2. 2
    Hook the index finger of your dominant hand through the O.
  3. 3
    State your question or hold the item. Take a breath.
  4. 4
    Try to pull your index finger through the O ring using a steady, consistent pull.
  5. 5
    Ring holds = YES (strong). Ring breaks open easily = NO (weak).

Practice these three together

Use the same baseline statements across all three methods. Notice which method gives you the clearest response - that is your body's preferred communication channel. Honour that.

Method 03 - Bonus

The Pendulum

Muscle testing with a visual amplifier - the same body intelligence, made visible

The pendulum is an extension of the body's energy system, not separate from it. The movement is driven by micro-muscle responses in your hand - the same autonomic signals, expressed through an external object. Think of it as muscle testing with a visual amplifier.

Programming your pendulum
  1. 1
    Hold the chain or cord between your thumb and index finger. Elbow slightly bent, hand steady.
  2. 2
    Let it settle to stillness. Ask: "Show me YES." Wait. Note the movement - clockwise circle, forward/back swing, or side-to-side. Whatever comes is yours.
  3. 3
    Let it settle again. Ask: "Show me NO." Note and remember.
  4. 4
    Ask "Show me MAYBE" or "Not ready to answer" - many pendulums give a diagonal or very faint movement here.
  5. 5
    Test on known statements to calibrate before asking new questions.
Circle = Yes (for many people)
Back/forth = No
Diagonal = Maybe
The same rules apply

Hydrate. Ground. Baseline first. Ask clear yes/no questions. The pendulum does not have special access to truth - your body does. The pendulum just makes the response visible. If you are in a highly emotional state, put it down and come back when you are settled.

Note from Mila: I did not use a pendulum for years because I thought it seemed "too out there." Then I tried it on a practical question and the answer was so clear it stopped me cold. Use what works for you. Leave the rest.

Tips for Accurate Results

What Mila comes back to every single time something feels off in her testing

After 30 years of bodywork and years of energy work, these are the things I come back to every single time something feels off in my testing.

Stay hydrated

Your body is a conductor. Dehydration disrupts the electrical signal. Drink a full glass of water before you test - this alone fixes most accuracy problems.

Get grounded first

Bare feet on the floor, three deep breaths, both feet hip-width apart. You are not just settling your mind - you are resetting your body's baseline electrical state.

Establish baseline every time

Do not skip this. Before every session, test one true and one false statement to confirm your signals are clear and calibrated for today's body, not yesterday's.

Do not overthink the question

Your conscious mind will try to predict the answer. Ask your question once, clearly, then release attachment to what comes back. The subconscious mind is faster than opinion.

Practice daily - even 5 minutes

Accuracy builds with repetition. Test mundane things: "Is this the right supplement today?" "Does my body want rest or movement?" Small questions build trust.

Clear your energy first

If you have been in a difficult conversation, absorbing someone else's energy, or feeling emotionally stirred - pause and clear before testing. Your field needs to be yours.

Do not test when exhausted

Late at night when the nervous system is depleted, signals become muddy. If you are getting confusing results, stop. Rest. Test again when you are resourced.

Ask specific, binary questions

"Is this good for me?" is harder to test than "Does my body need this supplement today?" Specific, present-tense, yes/no questions give the clearest answers.

Section 02

Everyday Applications

Once you trust your signals, you will use this a dozen times a day

Muscle testing is not reserved for deep healing sessions. Once you trust your signals, you will find yourself using it a dozen times a day - quietly, practically, without drama.

Food + Supplements

Hold a supplement or food and ask your body if it is right for you today. Bodies change. What tested well last month may not be needed now.

  • Morning supplement tray - test each one
  • "Is my body ready for this food right now?"
  • "Does this serving size feel right?"

Decisions + Choices

Not every decision needs logic. Some decisions need body wisdom. Test whether something is aligned for you, not just whether it looks good on paper.

  • "Is this the right time for this decision?"
  • Between two options - test each one separately
  • "Is this for my highest good?"

Trapped Emotions

Trapped emotions are energetic imprints stored in the body from past experiences. Muscle testing lets you locate and identify them so they can be released.

  • "Is there a trapped emotion affecting X?"
  • Use the Emotion Code chart to identify
  • "Is this emotion ready to be released?"

Energy + Body Check-ins

Your body has information your calendar does not. A quick morning check-in can shift an entire day.

  • "Does my body need rest or movement today?"
  • "Is this person's energy draining for me?"
  • "Is my energy field clear right now?"
Animal + Surrogate Testing

You can muscle test on behalf of anyone who cannot test themselves - children, animals, even plants. You hold the surrogate intention (I am testing on behalf of [name/pet]) and use your own body as the conduit. The subconscious mind knows how to hold that proxy connection. Results are often startlingly clear.
