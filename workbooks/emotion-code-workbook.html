What is the Emotion Code?

Developed by Dr. Bradley Nelson, D.C.

The Emotion Code is an energy healing method developed by Dr. Bradley Nelson, a chiropractor who spent decades observing a pattern in his patients: physical symptoms that had no clear physical cause - and that disappeared when the emotional energy behind them was identified and released.

The Core Principle

Emotional energy can become trapped in the body during intense or overwhelming experiences. These trapped emotions create energetic imbalances that can contribute to physical pain, emotional reactivity, relationship difficulties, and patterns that seem impossible to shift - no matter how much work you do.

Trapped emotions are real, measurable energies. They are not metaphor. They occupy space in your body, and they can distort the energy field of the tissue or organ where they are lodged.

How it works
  • We use muscle testing to communicate with the subconscious mind
  • The subconscious identifies the specific trapped emotion
  • We may gather details - the age it was trapped, whether it is inherited
  • We release it using magnetic energy along the governing meridian
What it can help with
  • Chronic physical pain with no clear cause
  • Anxiety, sadness, patterns of fear
  • Relationship difficulties
  • Feeling stuck or unable to move forward
  • The sense that something is blocking you
The Governing Meridian

The governing meridian runs from the tailbone, up the spine, over the top of the head and down the face to the upper lip. In traditional Chinese medicine, it governs all of the yang meridians. Running a magnet or your hand along this line three times is what releases the trapped emotional energy - essentially giving the subconscious the message that the emotion has been acknowledged and can now go.

Section 03

Understanding Muscle Testing

How the subconscious communicates through the body

Your subconscious mind processes everything - every experience, every emotion, every sensation you have ever had. It does not use words. It communicates through the body. Muscle testing is how we ask.

When a true statement is made, the body becomes stronger. When a false statement is made, or something resonates with an unresolved energy, the body becomes temporarily weaker. This is not a trick - it is the same principle used in kinesiology, and your body does it automatically whether you intend it to or not.

"Your body knows. It has always known. Muscle testing is just how we ask."

- Mila Velazquez
Three Methods for Self-Testing
Sway Test

Stand relaxed, feet shoulder-width apart. Make a true statement - your body will gently sway forward. Make a false statement - it will sway back. This is your baseline yes/no signal.

Arm Test

Extend one arm to the side at shoulder height. A partner applies gentle downward pressure on the wrist. A true statement - the arm holds strong. A false statement - the arm gives way.

Finger Test

Form a ring with your thumb and one finger. Try to pull the ring apart while making your statement. Strong ring = yes (true). Ring pulls apart easily = no (false). Good for solo practice.

Important

Muscle testing is a skill. It takes practice to trust it, especially on yourself. Hydration helps. If you are getting inconsistent signals, drink water and try again. Testing on simple, known truths first (your name, your birthday) builds confidence.

The Release Process

Step by step - follow this every time

The process is simple. Your subconscious does the heavy lifting. Your job is to ask the right questions in the right order, stay present, and trust what you receive.

  1. 1
    Establish your baseline Say your name and notice the sway forward (or a strong muscle test response). Say a false name and notice the movement back. This is your yes/no signal for the session.
  2. 2
    Ask: "Do I have a trapped emotion that my body is ready to release?" Wait for a clear yes or no response. If yes, continue. If no, ask if there is something else your body needs first.
  3. 3
    Identify the column Ask: "Is this emotion in Column A?" If yes - Column A. If no - Column B.
  4. 4
    Identify the row Ask: "Is it in rows 1, 2, or 3?" Narrow down to the specific row using yes/no questions.
  5. 5
    Narrow to the specific emotion Read through the emotions in that row one at a time. Your body will give a clear yes when you land on the right one.
  6. 6
    Ask for details (optional but valuable) Ask: "How old was I when this was trapped?" Count down in decades, then years. Ask: "Is this an inherited emotion?" (passed down from a parent or ancestor). If inherited, ask: "Was it from my mother's side or father's side?"
  7. 7
    Release the emotion Intend to release the trapped emotion. Run a magnet (or your hand, or a finger) firmly along the governing meridian - from the centre of your forehead, over the top of your head, and down the back of your neck - three times for a personal emotion, ten times for an inherited emotion.
  8. 8
    Confirm the release Ask: "Was that trapped emotion successfully released?" You should receive a clear yes. If not, repeat the release.

Governing meridian reminder: Start at the centre of your forehead. Run your hand (or magnet) over the crown of your head, continuing firmly down the back of your neck to the base of your skull. That is one pass. Do this 3 times for a standard release, 10 times for an inherited emotion.

What is a Heart-Wall?

One of the most significant discoveries in energy healing

When we experience deep heartache - loss, betrayal, grief, or prolonged emotional pain - the subconscious mind can take action to protect us. It builds what Dr. Nelson calls a Heart-Wall: a barrier around the heart made up of trapped emotional energy.

In the short term, this protection makes sense. It shields us from the full weight of pain we were not equipped to process at the time. But over time, a Heart-Wall becomes a wall - and it blocks not only pain coming in, but love going out and coming in as well.

Signs you may have a Heart-Wall
  • Difficulty feeling genuine joy or love even when life is objectively good
  • Feeling disconnected from people you care about
  • Relationships that consistently feel flat, effortful, or just slightly out of reach
  • A persistent sense of loneliness even in the company of others
  • Difficulty receiving love, compliments, or support gracefully
  • A feeling that your heart is somehow guarded - even when you do not want it to be
The Heart-Wall removal process

Heart-Wall removal works the same way as releasing any trapped emotion - but there are multiple layers. Each layer is a different trapped emotion that contributed to the wall. Most people need several sessions to clear a Heart-Wall completely. The results are often profound.

The subconscious will often describe the Heart-Wall as a material - wood, stone, brick, glass. This is not imagination. It is the subconscious communicating texture and density.

After Your Session

What to expect in the 1-3 days after releasing trapped emotions

Your body just did something significant. Releasing trapped emotions is real work - even though it feels gentle. Give yourself some care in the hours and days that follow.

The Processing Period

A processing period of 1 to 3 days after releasing is completely normal and is actually a sign that something real happened. This is not a bad sign - it is your body integrating the change. Stay with it.

Common experiences during processing
Emotional release You may feel waves of the released emotion - tears, sadness, even unexpected relief. This is the energy completing its exit. Let it move through you.
Tiredness Energy work is genuinely tiring. Rest if you can. Your nervous system has been recalibrated.
Vivid dreams Your subconscious often continues processing during sleep. Dreams may be intense, meaningful, or just strange. Write them down if you can.
Feeling lighter Many people describe a literal physical sensation of weight lifting. This is real. The energy that was trapped had density.
Temporary echoes You may briefly feel the emotion you released more intensely before it clears completely. This passes. It does not mean the release did not work.
Physical shifts Tingling, warmth, or changes in areas of chronic tension are common. Your body is reorganising.
Post-session self-care
  • Drink water. Lots of it. Energy work is dehydrating and hydration supports the release process.
  • Rest when you can. Avoid scheduling something demanding immediately after a session.
  • Be gentle with yourself. This is not the day for hard conversations or big decisions.
  • Note what comes up. Emotions, memories, dreams, or physical sensations that arise in the following days are often connected to what was released.
  • Trust the process. Your subconscious knows what it is doing.
