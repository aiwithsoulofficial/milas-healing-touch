The Scientific / Physical Side of Candles

10 Minutes

What physically happens:

  • Flame melts wax → liquid travels up wick → heat vaporises wax → vapour reacts with oxygen → creates flame
  • Basic combustion: fuel + heat + oxygen = heat and light
  • Flames move due to convection
Say This

"Even physically, the candle is a perfect metaphor for transformation. The solid becomes liquid. The liquid becomes vapour. The vapour becomes flame. Spiritually, this mirrors what happens in healing: what is dense begins to soften, move, release, and transform."

Why flames move:

  • Physical: air currents, drafts, uneven wick, impurities, moisture
  • Metaphysical: energetic activity, emotional resistance, spirit communication, clearing in progress
Key Line

"Not every dancing flame means spirit is talking. Sometimes your AC vent is talking. We have to learn how to hold both truths: the physical world and the energetic world. Discernment is spiritual maturity."

→

"So with that discernment in mind, let's learn the language of candle flames..."

How to Read Candle Flames Metaphysically

15 Minutes
Open With

"Candle reading is symbolic language. It is not about fear, obsession, or rigid rules. It is about noticing patterns, energy, and intuitive messages."

  • Strong, steady flame - aligned energy, clear intention, strong spiritual support. "The path is clear. Energy is stable."
  • Tall flame - strong energy, heightened spiritual activity, amplified intention. "This work is active." (Physical: wick too long / too much fuel)
  • Small or weak flame - low momentum, fatigue, resistance, blocked intention. "The energy may need more clarity, support, or clearing." (Physical: short wick, poor oxygen)
  • Flickering or dancing - energetic movement, spiritual communication, resistance shifting. "Something is moving." (Physical: airflow)
  • Popping or crackling - emotional charge, resistance, release of tension. "Something wants to be heard." (Physical: trapped moisture, debris)
  • Flame going out - resistance, timing not aligned, spiritual pause, redirection
Say This

"A candle going out is not automatically a curse, attack, or doom. Sometimes it means pause. Sometimes it means clear first. Sometimes it means the work needs to be approached differently."

→

"The flame is one message - but the wax tells its own story too. Let's look at what the wax, drips, and soot are showing us..."

The Wax, Drips, Soot & Shapes

15 Minutes

Wax represents:

Emotional residue, energetic discharge, subconscious material, how energy is being processed

  • Burns clean - smooth energy flow, aligned intention, minimal resistance
  • Excessive dripping - emotional overflow, unresolved heaviness, grief, release in progress
  • Heavy to one side - imbalance, internal conflict
        Left = receiving, feminine, subconscious, past
        Right = action, masculine, conscious, future
        (Teach as symbolic, not dogma)

Wax shapes - ask: "What does this remind me of?"

  • Heart = love, healing, heart chakra
  • Spiral = cycle, evolution, energy movement
  • Tears/drops = grief, emotional release
  • Snake = transformation, shedding
  • Flower = opening, heart expansion
  • Cross/X = crossroads, ending, divine support
  • Circle = completion, protection, wholeness
  • Mountain = obstacle, strength building
  • Face/figure = spirit symbolism, ancestral message
Say This

"Wax shapes are not always literal prophecy. They are often symbolic mirrors for the subconscious and energetic field."

Black soot/smoke:

Dense energy clearing, emotional heaviness, resistance, stagnant patterns releasing.

Key Line

"Not every black mark is a spiritual war story. Sometimes the wick just needed trimming."

Module 12

Fire Safety & Workshop Close

5 Minutes

Safety reminders (read through quickly):

  • Never leave a candle unattended
  • Keep away from curtains, paper, pets, children, loose fabric
  • Stable, heat-safe surface
  • Trim wick to 1/4 inch before use
  • Avoid heavy drafts
  • Don't burn too long
  • Discernment with herbs, oils, dressings
  • Extinguish consciously and safely
Say This

"We honour the element of fire by respecting it."
